A brief history of Borobudur Temple

Borobudur Temple is the largest Buddhist temple in the world and is one of the architectural wonders of the Sailendra kingdom era, around the 8th and 9th centuries AD. This temple is located in Magelang, Central Java, Indonesia.

Borobudur Temple has a pyramid structure consisting of nine levels; three circular levels, followed by six square levels. Above it are the main stupas, numbering 72, each with a statue of Buddha inside.

The walls of this temple are decorated with reliefs that tell the life and teachings of Buddha, as well as various stories from the Jatakas (stories about Buddha's past lives). These reliefs provide an in-depth picture of the culture and life of society at that time.

Borobudur is a UNESCO world heritage site and a popular tourist destination in Indonesia. Apart from being a place of worship, this temple is also a symbol of Buddhist culture and history in Indonesia.
